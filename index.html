About

William Henry Gates III, born October 28, 1955, is an American business magnate, software developer, and philanthropist. He co-founded Microsoft Corporation in 1975, revolutionizing personal computing. Gates has held positions of chairman, CEO, and president at Microsoft. Since stepping down, he focuses on philanthropic work through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

Philanthropy

The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

Established in 2000, the foundation is one of the largest private foundations in the world, with an endowment of over $50 billion. It focuses on improving healthcare, reducing extreme poverty, and expanding educational opportunities.

Key Initiatives

  • Global health programs to eradicate diseases like polio and malaria
  • Education reform to improve U.S. schools
  • Agricultural development for farmers in developing countries
  • Climate change initiatives and clean energy solutions
